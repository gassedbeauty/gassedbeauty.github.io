Red Curry Dumpling Soup

Red Curry Dumpling Soup

A classic Thai red curry gets a little cozy makeover in this red curry dumpling soup — spicy, creamy, and loaded with flavor in every bite. It’s the perfect meal when you’re feeling under the weather or just want something warm and comforting but still exciting. The broth is rich with coconut milk and red curry paste, layered with fish sauce, lime, and a hint of sweetness. Then you top it all with pan-fried dumplings for the best texture contrast — soft, crispy, and soaking up all that broth. It’s super easy to make and so hearty.

LET'S TALK CURRY PASTE

Red curry paste is the heart of this dish, and I always go for Mae Ploy — it’s rich, spicy, and packed with authentic Thai flavor. You don’t need a ton to get a deep, flavorful base, just enough to build that signature warmth and color. Cooking it down with garlic and ginger in coconut oil helps toast the spices and bring out all the aromatic goodness.

Use Palm Sugar for Authentic Sweetness

Instead of brown sugar, I love using palm sugar here to keep the flavor more traditional. If you don’t have access to this ingredient, just use brown sugar as called for in the recipe. If you want to go the extra mile, use palm sugar. It melts beautifully into the broth and has a softer, more rounded sweetness that balances the heat from the curry paste and red pepper flakes. Just make sure to chop or shave it into smaller pieces so it melts evenly.

Thai Basil Makes All the Difference

For the herbs, Thai basil is a must. It’s not the same as sweet Italian basil — Thai basil has a slightly spicy, licorice-like flavor that holds up really well in hot soups and adds that signature aroma you’d expect in a Thai curry. Of course, if you cant find any thai basil, any old basil will get the job done!

Dumplings — Any Kind Works

You can use any dumplings or wontons you love for this. Chicken, veggie, shrimp, beef — whatever you have on hand. I usually grab some from the freezer section at the market. Steam them right in the soup for a softer texture, or pan-fry them for crispy bottoms that hold up beautifully in the broth. I always prefer the crispy method — you get a nice crunchy bite that’s still juicy inside.

While your soup is simmering, heat some neutral oil in a nonstick pan. Add the dumplings in a single layer and let them get golden brown on the bottom. Then add a splash of water, cover, and steam until fully cooked through. This gives you a crispy base with a tender top, and they soak up just enough broth without getting soggy.

If you’re in a rush, you can skip the pan-frying and boil them straight in the broth — still delicious, just a little softer. But if you have the extra 5 minutes, the crispiness is so worth it.

How to Build the Perfect Broth

To make this red curry dumpling soup, start by heating a drizzle of coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Once it’s warm, add freshly minced garlic and ginger paste and sauté until super fragrant — your kitchen will already smell amazing. Then spoon in some red curry paste (I always go for Mae Ploy) and let it cook down for a couple of minutes. This step is everything — it toasts the spices, deepens the flavor, and makes the base extra bold and aromatic. Once it’s nice and toasty, pour in the full-fat coconut milk and stir to combine, letting it bubble gently into a rich, creamy broth. Add in some water to thin out the soup but you can also add in your broth of choice — chicken or veggie both work great — and bring it to a simmer. Now’s the time to season: a splash of fish sauce for savoriness, a pinch of sugar to balance the heat, red pepper flakes or chopped red chili for spice, and a squeeze of lime juice for brightness.
When the broth is tasting just right, it’s time for the dumplings. You can drop them in straight from the freezer and let them cook through in the soup — super easy and no fuss. But if you want to level it up, you can also pan-fry the dumplings until golden and crispy, or steam them separately for a soft and juicy bite before adding them into the soup at the end.
Feel free to incorporate your favorite veggies into this recipe — bok choy, mushrooms, shredded carrots, or a handful of spinach right before serving for a pop of green. Let everything simmer together until the flavors are cozy and blended, then mix in fresh basil and cilantro. Ladle the soup into bowls. If you pan fried or steamed the dumplings, add them in now. For even more spice, drizzle chili oil if you want extra heat. Finish off with scallions and a squeeze of lime to enjoy!

RECIPE:

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup red curry paste
  • 1/2 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 13-ounce can coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup water or broth
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar or palm sugar
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes, or 1 finely chopped red chili (to taste)
  • salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped Thai basil
  • 1 tablespoon cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, optional garnish
  • chili oil, optional garnish
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 12 to 16 dumplings or wontons

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, melt the coconut oil. Once it’s warm, add the ginger paste and minced garlic. Sauté for a minute or two until fragrant, but don’t let it brown.
  2. Stir in the red curry paste and cook for about 2 minutes to bring out its full flavor.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk, water, fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes. Stir until everything is well combined, then bring it to a gentle simmer. Let it bubble for 5 to 7 minutes to allow the flavors to come together.
  4. Taste the broth first before adding any salt. Then season as needed. Adjust with more lime juice, sugar, or red pepper flakes depending on how tangy, sweet, or spicy you like it.
  5. While the soup simmers, heat neutral oil in a nonstick pan over medium heat. Add the dumplings or wontons in a single layer and cook until the bottoms are golden brown. Add a splash of water, cover, and steam until fully cooked through.
  6. Turn off the heat and stir in the Thai basil and cilantro.
  7. Ladle the hot soup into bowls and top with the crispy dumplings. Garnish with scallions or a drizzle of chili oil if you like a kick.
